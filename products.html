What We Offer

Practical, climate-smart solutions empowering farmers in Kakuma Refugee Camp

Climate-Smart Farming

Training farmers on drought-resistant crops, soil health, mulching, and sustainable food production suited for arid regions.

Water-Efficient Irrigation

Introduction to drip irrigation, water harvesting, and low-cost technologies that maximize limited water resources.

Kitchen & Community Gardens

Supporting refugee households to establish small gardens for nutrition, food security, and income generation.

Alternative Livelihoods

Training in mushroom farming, poultry keeping, and other climate-resilient agribusiness opportunities.

Farmer Capacity Building

Hands-on workshops, mentorship, and peer learning to strengthen farming skills and local leadership.

Environmental Conservation

Promoting tree planting, soil conservation, and sustainable land-use practices in Kakuma.

Market Linkages

Helping farmers access local markets, cooperatives, and fair pricing for their produce.
